    Right to File FIR

    Any citizen can file an FIR at the nearest police station. Police cannot refuse to register a cognizable offense.

    Women's Rights

    Women can file complaints at any police station. Female officers available for sensitive cases.

    Child Rights

    Special protections under POCSO Act. Child-friendly procedures for reporting abuse.

    Free Legal Aid

    Free legal assistance available through Legal Services Authorities (NALSA). Call 15100.

    How to File an FIR (First Information Report)

    1
    Visit the nearest police station with jurisdiction over the area where the crime occurred
    2
    Provide written complaint or verbally state the facts to the officer-in-charge
    3
    Get the FIR registered and obtain a free copy (Section 154 CrPC)
    4
    If police refuse, approach the Superintendent of Police or magistrate

    For crimes against women, you can file Zero FIR at any police station

    Need Legal Help? Contact National Legal Services Authority (NALSA) at 15100 or visit www.nalsa.gov.in
